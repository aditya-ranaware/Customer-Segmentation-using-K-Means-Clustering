Customer Segmentation using K-Means Clustering
📖 Overview

This project focuses on segmenting customers based on their income and spending behavior using the K-Means clustering algorithm. The goal is to identify distinct customer groups to help businesses make data-driven decisions.

🎯 Problem Statement

Businesses often struggle to understand customer behavior due to lack of proper segmentation. This project aims to group customers into meaningful clusters for better marketing and customer targeting.

🎯 Objective
Identify different customer segments
Analyze customer behavior
Improve business decision-making
Enable targeted marketing strategies
📊 Dataset

The dataset contains:

CustomerID
Gender
Age
Annual Income (k$)
Spending Score (1–100)
⚙️ Technologies Used
Python
Pandas
NumPy
Matplotlib / Seaborn
Scikit-learn
🔄 Project Workflow
Data Loading
Data Understanding
Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
Feature Selection
Feature Scaling
Elbow Method (Optimal K Selection)
K-Means Clustering
Visualization
Insights & Conclusion
📈 Output
Customers are segmented into 5 clusters based on behavior
Each cluster represents a unique type of customer
💡 Key Insights
High Income & High Spending → Premium Customers 💰
High Income & Low Spending → Potential Customers 🎯
Low Income & High Spending → Discount-driven buyers
Medium Income → Regular customers
Low Income & Low Spending → Low-value customers
🏁 Conclusion

K-Means clustering successfully identified meaningful customer segments. These insights can help businesses optimize marketing strategies and improve customer engagement.
